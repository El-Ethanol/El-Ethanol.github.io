Research

Current work

My research focuses on the quantum-classical boundary: how quantum systems interact with their environment and what this tells us about the measurement problem.

QFT

Collapse models in curved spacetime

Extending CSL and Diosi-Penrose models to relativistic settings, studying Lorentz invariance and causality constraints on stochastic dynamics.

Open systems

Decoherence & master equations

Derivation of Lindblad master equations for relativistic quantum fields coupled to thermal baths, with applications to semiclassical gravity.

Foundations

Quantum measurement problem

Formal analysis of interpretational frameworks — GRW, CSL, Diosi-Penrose — and their compatibility with quantum field theory on curved backgrounds.

Numerical

Monte Carlo methods in QFT

Numerical integration and stochastic simulation techniques for computing observables in non-perturbative regimes of open quantum field theories.
